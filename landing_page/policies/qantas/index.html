Price Drop Policy Overview

Qantas does not offer price-drop adjustments or price-match guarantees. After booking, options depend on fare class: Red e-Deal fares: non-refundable and non-changeable (most restrictive). Sale fares: limited changes with fee. Flex and Flexi fares: free changes, partially refundable. Any Time fares: fully flexible, fully refundable. The 24-hour US DOT rule applies for US-originating tickets. For Australian Consumer Law (ACL) protections, Australian-origin bookings may have additional rights.

✅ US DOT 24-Hour Cancellation Rule

For tickets originating in the United States, Qantas complies with the US DOT 24-hour cancellation rule: full refund within 24 hours of booking provided departure is at least 7 days away. Cancel via Manage Booking at qantas.com or call 1-800-227-4500. Applies to tickets purchased directly through Qantas — OTA purchases are subject to OTA policies.

The 24-hour window is your most powerful tool. If you spot a lower fare within 24 hours of booking, you are entitled to a full cash refund — use it.

Basic Economy / Lowest Fare Rules

Qantas Red e-Deal and Sale fares (lowest economy): non-refundable, non-changeable after the 24-hour window — full forfeiture on cancellation. Economy Flex: date changes permitted with fee; non-refundable. Economy Flexi: free date changes, credit on cancellation. Any Time: fully flexible changes and cancellation with full refund. Business Sale: changeable with fee, non-refundable. Business Flexi: free changes, refundable.

⚠️ Always check your fare conditions before booking. Lowest-price fares often have zero flexibility after the 24-hour window.

Qantas Points Award Ticket Policy

Qantas Frequent Flyer Classic award tickets: points redeposited upon cancellation. Cancellation fee: approximately AUD $50 (approx USD $32) per person. Bronze/Silver/Gold/Platinum members may receive reduced fees. Cancel at least 24 hours before departure. Qantas Points expire after 18 months of account inactivity — earn or redeem points to keep account active.

Step-by-Step: How to Claim a Price Drop Refund

  1. Step 1: Log in to qantas.com and navigate to Manage Booking
  2. Step 2: Enter booking reference and last name or log in with Frequent Flyer account
  3. Step 3: Review fare class — Red e-Deal, Sale, Flex, Flexi, or Any Time
  4. Step 4: For Flexi/Any Time fares: select Change Flight to rebook at the lower available fare
  5. Step 5: For Economy Flex fares: assess change fee vs savings — may still be worthwhile
  6. Step 6: For tickets within 24-hour US DOT window: cancel for full refund, then rebook at lower price
  7. Step 7: For refund requests on eligible tickets: submit at qantas.com/feedback-and-complaints
  8. Step 8: Call Qantas US at 1-800-227-4500 for assistance

💡 Insider Tips & Loopholes

  • If Qantas cancels your flight or changes schedule by 3+ hours, you are entitled to a full cash refund on any fare class — request cash explicitly
  • Australian Consumer Law (ACL) provides stronger consumer protections than US DOT for Australian-origin bookings — for Australia-booked tickets, ACL rights may exceed standard fare conditions
  • Qantas operates the LAX–SYD, LAX–MEL, JFK–SYD, and DFW–SYD routes — these long-haul fares fluctuate significantly and monitoring can yield $200–600 savings on $1,500+ tickets
  • Qantas and American Airlines have a Joint Business Agreement on transpacific routes — AA Advantage miles can sometimes be used on QF flights, offering more flexibility for award bookings
  • US DOT 24-hour rule applies for all US-originating Qantas tickets — useful for the full refund window even on Red e-Deal fares
  • Qantas Points never expire as long as you earn or redeem at least once every 18 months — a small gift card purchase through Qantas Shopping keeps points alive
