Price Drop Policy Overview

Air France does not offer a price-match or price-drop adjustment guarantee. Options depend on fare class: Light/Standard economy fares: non-refundable, non-changeable (Light) or changeable with fee (Standard). Flex fares: free changes, refundable with fee. Full Flex: fully refundable. EU261/2004 Regulation: for flights originating in EU or operated by Air France to EU destinations, passengers have significant rights on cancellations and delays — not directly applicable to price drops but relevant to schedule changes.

✅ US DOT 24-Hour Cancellation Rule

For tickets purchased in the United States originating in the US, Air France complies with the US DOT 24-hour cancellation rule. Full refund within 24 hours provided the departure date is at least 7 days away. Cancel via My Bookings at airfrance.us or call 1-800-237-2747. Note: tickets purchased through OTAs are subject to the OTA's cancellation policy.

The 24-hour window is your most powerful tool. If you spot a lower fare within 24 hours of booking, you are entitled to a full cash refund — use it.

Basic Economy / Lowest Fare Rules

Air France Light fares (lowest economy): non-refundable, non-changeable after purchase — no flexibility whatsoever. Standard fares: changeable with fee (typically €150–200); non-refundable. Flex fares: free date changes, refundable with €150 fee. Full Flex: free changes and free cancellation with full cash refund. Business Light vs Business Flex: significant difference — always check at booking.

⚠️ Always check your fare conditions before booking. Lowest-price fares often have zero flexibility after the 24-hour window.

Flying Blue Award Ticket Policy

Flying Blue award tickets (Air France/KLM joint program): miles redeposited upon cancellation. Redeposit fee: approximately €45 per person for Classic members. Silver/Gold/Platinum Elite members receive reduced or waived fees. Cancel online at airfrance.com or call Flying Blue. Miles expire after 24 months of account inactivity — any earning or redemption resets the clock.

Step-by-Step: How to Claim a Price Drop Refund

  1. Step 1: Log in to airfrance.us and navigate to My Bookings
  2. Step 2: Enter booking reference and last name or log in with Flying Blue account
  3. Step 3: Review fare conditions — Light, Standard, Flex, or Full Flex determines your options
  4. Step 4: For Flex/Full Flex fares: click Modify My Flight and rebook at the lower fare
  5. Step 5: For Standard fares: calculate whether change fee + new fare is less than the original price
  6. Step 6: For cancellations within 24-hour US DOT window: cancel for full refund, then rebook
  7. Step 7: For EU261 rights on eligible EU-origin or Air France-operated EU flights: file claim at airfrance.com/en/common/guidedereclamation/
  8. Step 8: Call Air France US at 1-800-237-2747 for complex requests — have booking reference ready

💡 Insider Tips & Loopholes

  • EU261/2004 Regulation: if your Air France flight from a EU airport is canceled or delayed 3+ hours, you are entitled to €250–600 cash compensation per person — this is a legal right, not a goodwill gesture
  • Air France schedule change of 2+ hours on any ticket class entitles you to a full cash refund — always request cash, not an eVoucher
  • Air France and KLM share the Flying Blue program — miles earned on either airline count toward status and can be redeemed on both, effectively doubling your monitoring opportunity on transatlantic routes
  • Paris CDG routes from US East Coast (JFK, BOS, EWR, IAD) drop significantly in Jan/Feb and Oct/Nov — peak monitoring windows for $150–400 savings
  • Air France Full Flex upgrade cost at booking is often recovered on a single price drop or schedule change — worth calculating for bookings over $700
  • Book directly at airfrance.us — OTA bookings can prevent access to direct airline refund processing and EU261 claims
