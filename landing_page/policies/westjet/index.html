Price Drop Policy Overview

WestJet does not offer a price-drop adjustment or price-match guarantee. After booking, options depend on fare bundle: Econo: non-refundable, changeable with fee. EconoFlex: free changes, partially refundable. Premium: changeable, partially refundable. Business: free changes, fully refundable. Canadian Air Passenger Protection Regulations (APPR) provide strong rights for flight disruptions — stronger than US DOT for Canadian-origin flights. The 24-hour rule applies for US-originating WestJet bookings.

✅ US DOT 24-Hour Cancellation Rule

For tickets originating in the United States, WestJet complies with the US DOT 24-hour cancellation rule. For Canadian-origin tickets, Canadian APPR provides similar protections plus stronger disruption rights. Cancel via Manage Trips at westjet.com or call 1-888-937-8538. Note: WestJet and Air Canada are Canada's two major carriers — monitoring both on Canada-US routes can yield best-price tracking.

The 24-hour window is your most powerful tool. If you spot a lower fare within 24 hours of booking, you are entitled to a full cash refund — use it.

Basic Economy / Lowest Fare Rules

WestJet Econo bundle: non-refundable; changeable with fee (typically CAD $100–$200 depending on route); no WestJet Rewards points earned in some cases. EconoFlex: free changes (including same-day); cancellation gives WestJet Travel Bank credit. Premium: changeable for free, partially refundable with fee. Business: fully flexible and refundable. Fare differences on changes must be paid — no downgrade refund.

⚠️ Always check your fare conditions before booking. Lowest-price fares often have zero flexibility after the 24-hour window.

WestJet Dollars Award Ticket Policy

WestJet Rewards (WestJet Dollars) redemptions: WestJet Dollars redeposited upon cancellation at no fee for EconoFlex, Premium, and Business bundles. Econo bundle cancellation: WestJet Dollars may be forfeited depending on fare rules. WestJet Dollars never expire as long as account remains active with at least one flight per 24 months. WestJet Rewards status members (Silver/Gold) receive additional change/cancel flexibility.

Step-by-Step: How to Claim a Price Drop Refund

  1. Step 1: Log in to westjet.com and navigate to Manage Trips
  2. Step 2: Enter booking reference and last name or sign in with WestJet Rewards account
  3. Step 3: Review your fare bundle — Econo, EconoFlex, Premium, or Business
  4. Step 4: For EconoFlex/Premium/Business fares: select Change Flight and rebook at the lower available fare
  5. Step 5: For Econo fares: calculate change fee + fare difference vs original ticket price
  6. Step 6: For tickets within 24-hour US DOT window (US-origin): cancel for full refund, rebook
  7. Step 7: For Canadian APPR claims (Canada-origin): file disruption claim at westjet.com/en-ca/help/
  8. Step 8: Call WestJet at 1-888-937-8538 for assistance

💡 Insider Tips & Loopholes

  • Canadian APPR (Air Passenger Protection Regulations) for Canada-origin WestJet flights: stronger than US DOT — compensation of CAD $125–$1,000 for significant delays, plus full refund rights on large schedule changes
  • WestJet Travel Bank credits are valid 24 months — double the typical 12-month credit window at most US airlines, reducing urgency pressure when rebooking
  • WestJet Dollars never expire (as long as one flight every 24 months) — unlike airline miles, they don't erode over time
  • If WestJet significantly changes your schedule (typically 3+ hours), you are entitled to a full cash refund regardless of fare class under APPR
  • US DOT 24-hour rule applies for all US-originating WestJet tickets — WestJet operates YYZ/YVR/YYC routes from multiple US cities
  • WestJet EconoFlex bundle is often only $20–40 more than Econo — the zero-change-fee benefit can easily pay for itself on a single schedule adjustment or price-drop rebook
