Price Drop Policy Overview

Hawaiian Airlines does not offer a formal price-drop adjustment or price-match guarantee. Note: Hawaiian Airlines was acquired by Alaska Airlines (closed January 2025) — the two airlines continue to operate separately for now with separate policies, though integration is ongoing. Main Street Fares (basic economy): non-refundable, non-changeable. Main Cabin: changeable with fee. Flexible Fares: free changes, partially refundable. First Class: fully refundable. Monitor the HA/AS merger progress as policies may change.

✅ US DOT 24-Hour Cancellation Rule

Hawaiian Airlines complies with the US DOT 24-hour cancellation rule: full refund within 24 hours of booking provided departure is at least 7 days away. Cancel via Manage Trip at hawaiianairlines.com or call 1-800-367-5320. Post-merger with Alaska Airlines, check for potential integration of cancellation policies under the Alaska brand.

The 24-hour window is your most powerful tool. If you spot a lower fare within 24 hours of booking, you are entitled to a full cash refund — use it.

Basic Economy / Lowest Fare Rules

Hawaiian Airlines Main Street Fare (basic economy): non-refundable, non-changeable after 24-hour window — full forfeiture on cancellation. No seat selection, boarding last, no same-day changes. Main Cabin (standard economy): changeable with fee (currently $0 for most routes post-merger announcement). Flexible: free changes, partially refundable. First Class: fully refundable. Note: Hawaiian-Alaska merger may simplify or change these policies — check current terms at booking.

⚠️ Always check your fare conditions before booking. Lowest-price fares often have zero flexibility after the 24-hour window.

HawaiianMiles Award Ticket Policy

HawaiianMiles award tickets: miles redeposited upon cancellation. Cancellation fee: approximately $25–50 per person. Pualani Gold/Platinum members receive reduced or waived fees. As of January 2025 merger close, HawaiianMiles integration with Alaska Mileage Plan is ongoing — check current transfer and redemption options. Miles expire after 18 months of inactivity.

Step-by-Step: How to Claim a Price Drop Refund

  1. Step 1: Log in to hawaiianairlines.com and navigate to Manage Trip
  2. Step 2: Enter your booking confirmation number and last name
  3. Step 3: Review your fare type — Main Street, Main Cabin, Flexible, or First Class
  4. Step 4: For Flexible/First Class fares: select Change Flight to rebook at the lower available fare
  5. Step 5: For Main Cabin fares: check if change fee has been waived under current policy — post-merger many fees eliminated
  6. Step 6: For tickets within 24-hour US DOT window: cancel for full refund, rebook at lower price
  7. Step 7: For refund requests: submit at hawaiianairlines.com/customer-support/refunds
  8. Step 8: Call Hawaiian Airlines at 1-800-367-5320 for assistance

💡 Insider Tips & Loopholes

  • Hawaiian-Alaska merger (closed Jan 2025) means change/cancel fees have been eliminated on most Main Cabin fares — double-check current policy as this changes the math on price-drop recovery significantly
  • If Hawaiian Airlines cancels or significantly changes your flight schedule, you are entitled to a full cash refund on any fare class
  • Interisland Hawaii routes (HNL to Maui, Kauai, Big Island) price fluctuate frequently — monitoring these short-haul routes can yield $20–60 savings that add up over multiple travelers
  • US DOT 24-hour rule applies for all US-originating Hawaiian tickets — the 24-hour window is particularly valuable on Main Street fares that have zero flexibility afterward
  • HawaiianMiles may be converting to Alaska Mileage Plan — any pending miles balance may transfer; monitor official HA/AS communications for conversion announcements
  • Alaska Airlines codeshares on some Hawaiian routes post-merger — booking via Alaska may provide access to Alaska's refund and change policies which are generally more generous
