✈️ Why Southwest Has the Best Price Drop Policy

Southwest Airlines is unique among US carriers: zero change fees and zero cancellation fees on every single fare type. If the price drops after you book, you simply cancel and rebook at the lower price — the fare difference is returned to you as Travel Funds (or cash, for Anytime/Business Select fares).

There is no Basic Economy equivalent at Southwest. Even the cheapest Wanna Get Away fares can be cancelled with no fee — the full amount is issued as Travel Funds valid for 12 months.

Anytime and Business Select fares are fully refundable to your original payment method at any time. These are Southwest’s equivalent of refundable tickets on other carriers.

✅ Bottom line: If Southwest’s price drops even $1, it’s always worth cancelling and rebooking. There is never a fee or penalty for doing so on any fare.

Official policy: southwest.com — Manage Booking

⚡ The 24-Hour Rule

Under DOT regulations, Southwest provides a full cash refund for all fare types if you cancel within 24 hours of booking, as long as the flight departs at least 7 days away.

However, since Southwest’s policy is already $0 cancellation fees on all fares, the DOT 24-hour rule is less critical here than at other airlines. The main advantage is receiving cash back (rather than Travel Funds) for Wanna Get Away cancellations within 24 hours.

📋 Step-by-Step: Claim a Southwest Price Drop

  1. Go to southwest.com — Manage Booking and enter your confirmation number and passenger first & last name.
  2. Verify the current lower fare on southwest.com. Because Southwest doesn’t show on Google Flights or most OTAs, always check directly on southwest.com for accurate pricing.
  3. Click "Change" or "Cancel" on the booking. Select “Cancel Flight” to receive Travel Funds.
  4. Travel Funds are issued immediately to the booking confirmation number. For Anytime or Business Select fares, a full cash refund is returned to your original payment method.
  5. Search for and book the same flight at the new lower price. At checkout, select "Apply Travel Funds" and enter the original confirmation number to apply the credit.
  6. Travel Funds can be applied to bookings for any Southwest passenger — not just the original traveler. The confirmation number holder can apply the funds to another person’s ticket.
  7. Phone: 1-800-435-9792 available 24/7. The Southwest app also fully supports cancellations and rebooking.

💡 Insider Tips for Southwest Price Drops

  • Check southwest.com directly — not Google Flights: Southwest does not participate in GDS systems, so prices on Kayak, Google Flights, and Expedia are either not shown or inaccurate. Always check southwest.com for real pricing.
  • Wanna Get Away Plus is worth the upgrade: The small premium over Wanna Get Away buys you transferable Travel Funds — a major advantage if your travel plans change or you want to gift the credit to a travel companion.
  • Travel Funds work for ANY passenger: Unlike credits at most airlines, Southwest Travel Funds can be applied to any passenger’s booking as long as you have the original confirmation number. Great for group travel or family trip coordination.
  • Book Anytime fare if flexibility is critical: For trips where you might need to cancel last minute, Anytime fare’s cash refund policy is worth the premium over Wanna Get Away’s Travel Funds.
  • Airline-cancelled flights = full cash refund: If Southwest cancels or significantly changes your flight, you receive a full cash refund on any fare type — not just Travel Funds. Call to request it explicitly.
  • Rapid Rewards: no redeposit fee, ever: Points are refunded instantly with zero fees when you cancel an award ticket. No other major US carrier offers this policy across all status levels.

💳 Credit Card Price Protection as a Backup

If the airline won’t cover a price drop, your travel credit card may offer a secondary layer of protection.

Trip Cancellation / Interruption Insurance (offered by many premium travel cards) reimburses non-refundable costs when you cancel for a covered reason. Purchase Protection on select cards covers price drops on new purchases within 90 days.

Cards historically offering price protection include select Citi and Amex products. Always check your card’s current benefit guide — many issuers have reduced benefits in recent years.

⚠️ Credit card price protection has been discontinued by Chase, Citi, and others. Verify your specific card’s current terms before relying on this option.
