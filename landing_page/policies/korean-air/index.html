📋 Price Drop Policy Overview

Korean Air does not offer a formal price-drop refund or price-match guarantee. When the fare drops after you book, your options depend entirely on the fare class purchased:

⚠️ Saver/Promo fares: Non-refundable and non-changeable after the 24-hour US DOT window. If you book a Saver fare and the price drops, you cannot rebook at the lower price — the ticket value is forfeited.
✅ Standard/Flexible fares: Can be changed for a fee. You can cancel and rebook at the lower fare, paying the applicable change fee. Net savings = price drop minus fee.
✅ Fully Flexible/Business/First: Fully changeable and refundable. Cancel at any time and rebook at the lower price for full value recovery.

⚡ 24-Hour Full Refund Rule (US Tickets)

For tickets originating in the United States, Korean Air complies with the US Department of Transportation (DOT) 24-hour cancellation rule. You are entitled to a full refund to your original payment method if you cancel within 24 hours of booking, provided the departure is at least 7 days away.

💡 Korean Air operates nonstop from JFK, LAX, and SEA to Incheon (ICN), making these routes eligible for the US DOT 24-hour rule. Connecting US itineraries via ICN onward also qualify.

To use the 24-hour rule: Cancel via Manage My Reservation at koreanair.com or call 1-800-438-5000.

🪜 Step-by-Step Claim Process

  1. Log in to koreanair.com → Manage My Reservation using your booking reference and last name.
  2. Review your fare conditions — find your ticket's fare class (Saver, Standard, Flexible, etc.) and confirm if changes are permitted.
  3. Within 24h of booking (US origin): Cancel for a full refund. Then search for and book the same flight at the lower price.
  4. Standard fares (after 24h): Select "Change Flight" and pay the applicable change fee (approximately $100–200 depending on route) to rebook at the lower fare. Calculate net savings = price drop minus fee.
  5. Flexible fares (after 24h): Cancel for full refund or change to the lower-priced flight with no or minimal fee.
  6. For cash refund requests: complete the refund request form at koreanair.com/us/en/support.
  7. Refunds are processed within 7–14 business days to the original payment method.

🏆 SKYPASS Award Ticket Rules

SKYPASS award tickets can be cancelled with miles redeposited to your account. The redeposit fee is approximately $50 USD per person for non-elite members.

✅ SKYPASS Morning Calm members and above: Reduced or waived redeposit fees. Check your status before assuming full fees apply.

Taxes and fees paid on award tickets are refunded to the original payment method within 7–14 business days. Always cancel at least 24 hours before departure — no-shows forfeit all miles with no redeposit option.

💡 Insider Tips & Loopholes

  • Schedule change = full refund right: If Korean Air cancels your flight or changes your schedule by 2+ hours, you are entitled to a full cash refund on ANY fare class — always ask for cash, not a credit.
  • SkyTeam alliance protection: Korean Air is a SkyTeam member. Schedule changes by Delta or other SkyTeam partners on connecting itineraries may trigger Korean Air refund rights on your through-ticket.
  • Use the 24-hour window aggressively: The US DOT 24-hour rule applies for all US-originating Korean Air tickets. If you spot a lower fare within 24 hours of booking, cancel and rebook immediately.
  • IAD/JFK/LAX to ICN monitoring: Korean Air regularly adjusts fares on trans-Pacific routes 30–60 days out. TripReclaim monitoring has detected $100–400 drops on ICN routes.
  • Morning Calm status benefits: SKYPASS Morning Calm members get free or reduced award redeposit fees — check status before canceling an award ticket to avoid paying $50 unnecessarily.
  • Book directly at koreanair.com: OTA bookings (Expedia, Google Flights checkout) significantly complicate change/cancel requests. Always book direct to retain full refund rights.
