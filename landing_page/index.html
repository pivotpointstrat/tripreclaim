FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

How does TripReclaim track flight prices?
We continuously monitor airline pricing and compare your original booking price against current fares. When a qualifying price drop occurs, we instantly alert you with the exact steps to claim your refund.
How much can I realistically expect to recover?
Our monitored travelers recover an average of $142 per trip when a price drop is detected. Flight prices fluctuate constantly — sales, seat sales, and schedule adjustments cause drops all the time, especially 2–8 weeks after booking.
What airlines are supported?
We support all major US carriers: American Airlines, Delta, United, Southwest, JetBlue, Alaska, Spirit, Frontier, and more. We're expanding international airline coverage regularly.
How quickly will I be notified of a price drop?
Our system checks prices every hour. The moment a qualifying drop is detected, you receive an email alert (and SMS on monthly/annual plans) with step-by-step refund instructions.
What if my airline doesn't offer refunds for price drops?
Many credit cards offer price protection that's totally separate from airline policy. We provide guides for both airline-direct refunds AND credit card claims. You'd be surprised how often card benefits cover the gap.
Is my booking information secure?
Yes. We use 256-bit encryption and never store complete booking details or payment information. Only the minimum required data for price tracking is temporarily processed.
Can I cancel my subscription?
Absolutely. Monthly and annual plans can be cancelled anytime from your account dashboard. No questions asked, no penalty fees.
